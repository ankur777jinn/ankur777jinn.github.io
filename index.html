Ankur Dahiya

AI Researcher  ·  IIT Roorkee  ·  India

Hi, I'm Ankur. I'm an undergrad studying Data Science & Artificial Intelligence at IIT Roorkee, and a core member of the Vision and Language Group (VLG).

My research is about understanding how large models work internally and making them better aligned, more efficient, and more trustworthy. I'm particularly drawn to mechanistic interpretability — reverse-engineering the circuits and algorithms that neural networks learn — and to building RL alignment frameworks that go beyond scalar reward signals.

On the practical side, I care a lot about model pruning (we recently got a paper accepted at AAAI on evolutionary pruning of LLMs) and about the compute infrastructure that makes all this research possible — pipeline optimization, cluster health-checks, the stuff nobody talks about but everybody needs.

Outside of research, I like long walks, overthinking font choices, and occasionally submitting to the void that is academic peer review.

You can check out my publications, read my blog, or grab my resume ↓.
