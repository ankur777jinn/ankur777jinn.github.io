I am an undergraduate student in Data Science & Artificial Intelligence at the Indian Institute of Technology, Roorkee, and a core member of the Vision and Language Group (VLG). My research focuses on understanding and improving large-scale models through principled, mechanistic approaches—spanning interpretability, alignment, and efficient inference.

Research Interests

Mechanistic Interpretability

Reverse-engineering the computational structure of neural networks to understand learned algorithms and internal representations.

RL Alignment Frameworks

Developing reinforcement learning methods that improve policy optimization with human feedback and critique-augmented training signals.

Model Pruning

Designing structured and unstructured pruning strategies inspired by evolutionary selection to produce efficient, smaller models without sacrificing capability.

Compute Infrastructure

Optimizing training pipelines, multi-GPU cluster orchestration, and building reliable infrastructure for large-scale ML experiments.

CATPO: Critique-Augmented Tree Policy Optimization

Under Review

Ankur Dahiya, Umang Goyal, Ayush  ·  Submitted to COLM 2026

We propose CATPO, a novel RL alignment framework that augments tree-search policy optimization with structured critique feedback. By leveraging a critic model to generate fine-grained evaluations at each node of the policy tree, CATPO improves exploration efficiency and produces policies that are better aligned with human preferences compared to standard RLHF approaches.

Natural Selection of LLMs via Pruning

Accepted

Umang Goyal, Ankur Dahiya, Ayush  ·  AAAI 2026 Student Abstract Track

We draw an analogy between biological natural selection and neural network pruning to develop an evolutionary pruning framework for large language models. Our method iteratively evaluates subnetwork fitness, selects and mutates surviving structures, and produces compact models that retain competitive performance on downstream benchmarks while significantly reducing parameter count.
